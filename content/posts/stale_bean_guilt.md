I like to try a lot of whole-bean coffees. However, I donâ€™t want to buy too much just to throw some away. So when visiting SF last year, upon discovering [the Rainbow Grocery Cooperative](https://rainbow.coop) sold whole-bean coffee from named roasters _by weight_ rather than _by bag_, I made a day of it! I happily brought the haul (of mostly light-roast Ethiopian beans) all the way back to my office desk in NYC.

This year, I re-discovered the haul among the multiple bins beneath my overstuffed office desk. And with some guilt, I resolved to _get through_ the pouches of beans before getting any new whole-bean coffees.

I ended up making a lot of French press coffee to _get through_ those stale beans. Temporarily, a responsibility to prevent waste overshadowed the usual joyful comfort of brewing coffee. However, I finally _got through_ those stale beans. So now I can procure whole beans to start the cycle again (hopefully with less excess this time)!
