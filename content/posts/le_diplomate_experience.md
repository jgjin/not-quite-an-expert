Based on [these recommendations](https://www.reddit.com/r/washingtondc/comments/19fbbug/best_coffee_beans_in_dc), I followed procuring Lost Sock and Small Planes coffee beans into Logan Circle. Then I followed the recommendation of my coworker friend into [Le Diplomate](https://lediplomatedc.com), a French restaurant and neighborhood staple. I found each part of the meal interesting to discuss.

They seated me at a corner table near where the servers close out checks and gossip, less glamorous and more entertaining. Then they brought out their signature bread basket. I initially felt a base concern about the high carbs in the bread and the high fat in the butter; American [nutritionism](https://podcasts.happyscribe.com/maintenance-phase/snackwell-s-cookies#t2366-210) currently fixates on protein so much I can buy "protein water" at Target. However, recognizing the shortcomings of American nutritionism, I tried to enjoy the bread basket [as a French person would, rather than as an American person wouldn't](https://maintenancephase.buzzsprout.com/1411126/episodes/11092999-french-women-don-t-get-fat).

Next came the foie gras parfait. I know foie gras as [made by force-feeding birds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foie_gras#Animal_abuse) and as [originally accessible only to royalty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foie_gras#Cultural_significance_in_France). From my plate I could hear the cries of animal welfare activists and the echoes of [the Bastille](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Storming_of_the_Bastille). As if punished for my consumption of foie gras, I couldn't read the overly ornamental bathroom labels [prioritizing form over function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Form_follows_function). I had to induce the more suitable bathroom based on the outgoers.

I ended the meal with rabbit [pappardelle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pappardelle), because I index on novel dishes and rabbit is novel to me. The rabbit tasted like chicken and the pasta tasted like not-enough-sauce, so [advised by Julia Child](https://www.thekitchn.com/4-things-we-learned-about-butter-from-julia-child-228696), I added the remaining butter from the bread basket into the pasta.

The check catches me content with bread, butter, duck, and rabbit. Scanning the damage to my wallet, I realize French food is overrated.
